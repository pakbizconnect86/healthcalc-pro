Body Mass Index (BMI)

What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measurement that uses your height and weight to estimate whether you have a healthy body weight.

It is calculated as: weight(kg) ÷ height(m)²

BMI Categories:
🔵 Under 18.5 — Underweight
🟢 18.5 – 24.9 — Normal weight
🟡 25.0 – 29.9 — Overweight
🔴 30.0 and above — Obese

BMI is a screening tool, not a diagnostic tool. Always consult a doctor for a complete health assessment.

Daily Calories (TDEE)

Understanding Calories

TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is the number of calories you burn per day.

This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor Equation, the most accurate formula available today.

Use your results to:
🥗 Eat at TDEE to maintain weight
📉 Eat 500 kcal less to lose ~0.5kg/week
💪 Eat 300–500 kcal more to gain muscle

Macronutrient split: 40% carbs, 30% protein, 30% fat is a great starting point.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the BMI calculator?

Our BMI calculator uses the standard WHO formula (weight in kg divided by height in metres squared). It is clinically accurate for adults aged 18–65. However, BMI does not account for muscle mass, bone density, or fat distribution. Athletes may have a high BMI despite being very healthy. Always consult a doctor for a complete health evaluation.

What is a healthy BMI range?

According to the World Health Organization, a BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 is considered a healthy weight. A BMI below 18.5 indicates underweight, 25–29.9 is overweight, and 30 or above is classified as obese. These thresholds may differ slightly for Asian populations.

How many calories should I eat to lose weight?

To lose approximately 0.5 kg (1 lb) per week, aim to eat 500 calories below your TDEE (daily maintenance calories) calculated above. Avoid going below 1,200 kcal/day for women or 1,500 kcal/day for men without medical supervision, as extreme restriction can harm your metabolism.

How much water should I drink every day?

A common guideline is to drink 35 ml of water per kilogram of body weight daily. However, your actual needs vary based on activity level, climate, diet, and health conditions. Our water intake calculator gives you a personalized recommendation based on your specific situation.

Are these calculators free to use?

Yes — 100% free, forever. No account needed, no hidden fees, no premium tiers. We believe everyone deserves access to basic health information tools.

Can I use these calculators for children?

Standard BMI calculations for adults do not apply to children (under 18). For children, BMI must be interpreted using age- and gender-specific percentile charts. Our calculator is designed primarily for adults aged 18 and above.
